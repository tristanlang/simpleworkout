My History

I dabbled with many forms of exercise during high school and lifted a bit in college. During my senior year, I found CrossFit, which opened my eyes to many different aspects of fitness that I had never seen before. Through my CrossFit years, I became very serious about Olympic weightlifting, power lifting, and energy system training, each of which I focused on more heavily during various parts of six years.

After pondering the return I was getting on the time I spent in the gym, I decided that I would rather shift my purpose in training from a focus on performance to a focus on longevity. In the end, isn't this why we work out?

In my mind, this decision serves three purposes. The first is that high volumes of training (particularly strength training) had previuosly increased my hunger to levels that required nearly 5000 calories daily. Not only did this cause me to think of food almost non-stop, it also consisted of unhealthy eating practices.

This leads into the second purpose of this decision, which is to establish a lifetime of healthy eating practices. By being less focused on training, I can better focus on my nutrition and improve the quality of the foods that I consume.

Third, serious training takes serious time. I was spending roughly two hours in the gym each day quite often, but by shifting my training focus to longevity, I can spend more time on other aspects of life.

Methodology

In early 2013, I read an article at Eat Move Improve regarding training for general health and longevity. I generally agree with the principles enumerated there, which I will summarize here as the basis for my current training:

  • Cardiovascular work at 3+ times per week for a solid 20-60 minutes straight, with an average intensity of a 3 or 4 on the RPE scale of 1-10
  • Strength and flexibility work should be modeled towards the full squat and overhead press
  • Some form of pulling like rowing or pullups should be included for push/pull balance

With this in mind, I plan to make good use of my airdyne, 24kg kettlebells, pullup bar, and running for as long as I can. I will typically do lifts with kettlebells in the range of 8-12 reps, with roughly 2 minutes rest between sets. My rough plan is to do cardio every other day, with strength and circuits alternating between those days. When it gets nicer outside, I might substitute tennis, hiking, or other activities for some of the days.
